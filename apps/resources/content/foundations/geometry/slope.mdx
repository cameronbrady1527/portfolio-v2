How steep is a line, and which way does it lean? **Slope** answers both with one
number: the **rise** (vertical change) divided by the **run** (horizontal
change) between any two points on the line. It builds directly on
[points and quadrants](/foundations/geometry/points-and-quadrants) — you read two
points off the grid, then compare how much $y$ changes to how much $x$ changes.

<SkillCard skill="slope" title="Slope">

<SkillStep title="What & why">

Pick any two points on a line. The **slope** is how much it climbs for how much
it moves across:

$$m = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}} = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$$

The number carries two pieces of meaning at once:

- **Sign** — the *direction*. A line rising left-to-right has **positive** slope;
  one falling left-to-right has **negative** slope.
- **Size** — the *steepness*. A slope of $3$ is steeper than $\tfrac{1}{2}$.

Two special lines: a **horizontal** line never rises, so $\Delta y = 0$ and the
slope is $0$. A **vertical** line never runs, so $\Delta x = 0$ — and since you
can't divide by zero, its slope is **undefined**.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Watch how it works">

Take $(0, 0)$ and $(4, 2)$. Going from the first point to the second, $y$ rises
by $2$ and $x$ runs by $4$:

<CoordinateGrid
  points={[{ x: 0, y: 0, label: "A" }, { x: 4, y: 2, label: "B" }]}
  segment
  range={6}
  caption="From A to B the line rises 2 and runs 4 — slope = 2/4 = 1/2."
/>

$$m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{2 - 0}{4 - 0} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}$$

The order of the points doesn't matter as long as you stay consistent: from $B$
to $A$ the rise is $-2$ and the run is $-4$, and $\tfrac{-2}{-4}$ is still
$\tfrac{1}{2}$.

When the line **falls**, the slope comes out negative. From $(0, 5)$ to $(2, 1)$
the rise is $1 - 5 = -4$ over a run of $2$, so $m = \tfrac{-4}{2} = -2$ — a steep
downhill line.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Reason through one together">

Find $\Delta y$ and $\Delta x$, divide, and watch the sign. Predict before you
check.

<PredictThenCheck
  prompt="Find the slope of the line through (1, 4) and (3, 0)."
  choices={["-2", "2", "-1/2", "-4"]}
>

It's **−2**. The rise is $\Delta y = 0 - 4 = -4$ and the run is
$\Delta x = 3 - 1 = 2$, so $m = \tfrac{-4}{2} = -2$. Dropping the sign gives $2$
(but the line clearly falls); flipping rise and run gives $-\tfrac{1}{2}$; and
$-4$ is just $\Delta y$ — you still have to divide by the run.

</PredictThenCheck>

</SkillStep>
