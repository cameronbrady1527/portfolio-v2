If there's one move the Geometry Regents asks for over and over, it's this:
**substitute given values into a formula and evaluate.** Area, perimeter, the
Pythagorean theorem, slope — every one is a formula waiting for numbers. Do the
substitution carefully (especially with negatives) and the rest is the
[order of operations](/foundations/geometry/order-of-operations) you already
know. This card builds that care into a habit.

<SkillCard skill="substituting-into-formulas" title="Substituting into a Formula">

<SkillStep title="What & why">

A **formula** is an expression with letters standing in for numbers you'll fill
in later. $A = \ell w$ waits for a length and a width; $c^2 = a^2 + b^2$ waits for
two legs. **Substituting** just means replacing each letter with its value.

The one place it goes wrong is signs. When a variable is negative, wrap it in
parentheses as you substitute — $x = -4$ becomes $(-4)$, not a loose $-4$. That
keeps $\,3x\,$ reading as $3 \times (-4)$ and $\,x^2\,$ reading as $(-4)^2 = 16$,
not $-16$. Substitute with parentheses, then evaluate by the order of operations.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Watch how it works">

**A plain expression.** Evaluate $3x + 2$ when $x = 5$:

$$3x + 2 = 3(5) + 2 = 15 + 2 = 17$$

**A real formula.** The perimeter of a rectangle is $P = 2(\ell + w)$. With
$\ell = 7$ and $w = 3$, do the parentheses first:

$$P = 2(7 + 3) = 2(10) = 20$$

**Mind the negatives.** Evaluate $x^2 + 1$ when $x = -5$. The parentheses are
what save you:

$$(-5)^2 + 1 = 25 + 1 = 26$$

Without them, $-5^2$ would read as $-(5^2) = -25$ — a classic, avoidable slip.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Reason through one together">

Substitute with parentheses, then evaluate. A negative value is where most
mistakes hide — predict before you check.

<PredictThenCheck
  prompt="Evaluate 2x − 3 when x = −4."
  choices={["−11", "−5", "5", "11"]}
>

It's **−11**. Substitute with parentheses: $2(-4) - 3$. Multiply first:
$2 \times (-4) = -8$. Then $-8 - 3 = -11$. The trap is mishandling the sign —
treating $2x$ as $+8$, or stopping at $-8$. Parentheses around the $-4$ keep the
multiplication honest.

</PredictThenCheck>

</SkillStep>

</SkillCard>
