Before a single coordinate, slope, or length calculation in geometry will go
smoothly, one thing has to be automatic: **adding and subtracting signed
numbers**. Not "I can work it out if I slow down" — automatic, the way you know
$7 + 3$. This card builds that fluency from the idea up, then drills it until it
sticks.

<SkillCard skill="adding-subtracting-signed-numbers" title="Adding & Subtracting Signed Numbers">

<SkillStep title="What & why">

A **signed number** carries a direction as well as a size. Positive means one
way, negative the other — and they're everywhere you'll look in this course:

- A point at $x = -3$ sits three units *left* of the origin.
- A line going downhill has a *negative* slope.
- Cooling from $4°$ to $-2°$ is a drop you find by *subtracting*.

Think of a number line. Every number is a spot on it; adding and subtracting
just means **moving along it** — right for positive, left for negative. Get that
picture solid and the rules stop being rules you memorize and start being things
you can *see*.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Watch how it works">

**Adding a negative moves you left.** Take $5 + (-8)$. Start at $5$, then move
$8$ to the left. You pass $0$ and keep going three more, landing on $-3$:

$$5 + (-8) = -3$$

**Subtracting a negative moves you right** — it's the same as adding its
opposite. Take $-2 - (-6)$. Subtracting $-6$ is *adding* $6$, so start at $-2$
and move $6$ to the right, landing on $4$:

$$-2 - (-6) = -2 + 6 = 4$$

Two moves, two directions. That's the whole game: a positive step goes right, a
negative step goes left, and "subtract" flips the step's direction.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Reason through one together">

Here's the case that trips everyone up at first — subtracting a negative. Picture
the number line before you peek.

<PredictThenCheck
  prompt="Start at 3. What is 3 − (−5)?"
  choices={["8", "−2", "−8", "2"]}
>

It's **8**. Subtracting a negative is the same as adding its opposite, so
$3 - (-5)$ becomes $3 + 5$. Start at $3$ and move $5$ to the *right* — you land on
$8$. The two minus signs didn't cancel by magic; subtracting the negative $-5$
*reverses* its leftward pull into a rightward step.

</PredictThenCheck>

</SkillStep>

</SkillCard>
