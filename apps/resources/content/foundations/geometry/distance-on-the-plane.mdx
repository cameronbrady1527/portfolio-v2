How far apart are two points? When they share a row or column it's a simple
subtraction. When they sit diagonally, the segment between them is the hypotenuse
of a right triangle — so the **distance formula is the Pythagorean theorem in
disguise**. This card connects the two, building on
[square roots](/foundations/geometry/square-roots).

<SkillCard skill="distance-on-the-plane" title="Distance on the Coordinate Plane">

<SkillStep title="What & why">

If two points line up **horizontally** (same $y$) or **vertically** (same $x$),
the distance is just the difference of the coordinates that change — count the
units.

For a **diagonal**, drop a right triangle: the horizontal change $\Delta x$ and
the vertical change $\Delta y$ are the legs, and the distance is the hypotenuse.
That's exactly the Pythagorean theorem:

$$d = \sqrt{(\Delta x)^2 + (\Delta y)^2}$$

So "distance between two points" is never a new idea — it's $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$
applied to the grid.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Watch how it works">

Take $(0, 0)$ and $(3, 4)$. The horizontal change is $3$, the vertical change is
$4$ — the legs of a right triangle whose hypotenuse joins the points:

<CoordinateGrid
  points={[{ x: 0, y: 0, label: "A" }, { x: 3, y: 4, label: "B" }]}
  segment
  range={6}
  caption="Δx = 3 and Δy = 4 are the legs; the segment AB is the hypotenuse."
/>

$$d = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5$$

Axis-aligned points are the easy case: $(1, 2)$ to $(6, 2)$ share a $y$, so the
distance is just $|6 - 1| = 5$ — no triangle needed.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Reason through one together">

Find $\Delta x$ and $\Delta y$, then apply the Pythagorean theorem. Predict before
you check.

<PredictThenCheck
  prompt="Find the distance between (0, 0) and (6, 8)."
  choices={["10", "14", "48", "100"]}
>

It's **10**. The changes are $\Delta x = 6$ and $\Delta y = 8$, so
$d = \sqrt{6^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = 10$. Adding the legs
($6 + 8 = 14$) skips the theorem, and $100$ is $d^2$ — you still have to take the
square root.

</PredictThenCheck>

</SkillStep>

</SkillCard>
