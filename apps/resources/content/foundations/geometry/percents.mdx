A percent is just a **ratio out of 100**: $30\%$ means $30$ per hundred, or
$\frac{30}{100}$. That one idea — treat a percent as the proportion
$\frac{\text{part}}{\text{whole}} = \frac{\text{percent}}{100}$ — handles every
percent question you'll meet: a part, a percent, a whole, plus discounts, tax,
tips, and percent change. This card builds directly on
[ratios & proportions](/foundations/geometry/ratios-proportions).

<SkillCard skill="percents" title="Percents">

<SkillStep title="What & why">

"Per cent" literally means *per hundred*. So $\frac{\text{part}}{\text{whole}}$
and $\frac{\text{percent}}{100}$ are two names for the same ratio:

$$\frac{\text{part}}{\text{whole}} = \frac{\text{percent}}{100}$$

Every percent problem just hides one of those three pieces. Find the part
("what is $30\%$ of $60$?"), the percent ("$12$ is what percent of $48$?"), or
the whole ("$20\%$ of what is $30$?") — it's the same proportion each time, solved
by the cross-multiplication you already know.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Watch how it works">

**Finding the part.** $30\%$ of $60$: that's $\frac{30}{100} \times 60$.

$$\frac{30}{100} \times 60 = 0.30 \times 60 = 18$$

**Finding the percent.** Is $12$ what percent of $48$? Put it over the whole and
scale to $100$:

$$\frac{12}{48} = \frac{1}{4} = \frac{25}{100} = 25\%$$

**Everyday percents are the same setup.** A \$80 jacket at $25\%$ off: the
discount is $25\%$ of $80 = \$20$, so the sale price is $80 - 20 = \$60$. Tax and
tips work the same way — find the percent of the amount, then add it on.

</SkillStep>

<SkillStep title="Reason through one together">

For a discount, find the percent *of* the price first, then subtract. Predict
before you check.

<PredictThenCheck
  prompt="A $50 item is 20% off. What is the sale price?"
  choices={["$40", "$30", "$45", "$10"]}
>

It's **\$40**. First find the discount: $20\%$ of $50$ is
$\frac{20}{100} \times 50 = \$10$. The sale price is what's *left*:
$50 - 10 = \$40$. Stopping at \$10 (the discount itself, the common trap) answers
"how much off," not "how much you pay."

</PredictThenCheck>

</SkillStep>

</SkillCard>
