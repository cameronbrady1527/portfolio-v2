A linear **inequality** in two variables, like $y \le x + 3$, is solved not by a
single line but by a whole **half-plane** — every point on one side of the
boundary line. A **system** of inequalities asks for the points that satisfy *all*
of them at once: the **overlap** of the half-planes.

- The boundary is **solid** when the inequality includes equality ($\le$ or
  $\ge$) and **dashed** when it is strict ($<$ or $>$).
- To check a point, substitute it into **every** inequality — it is a solution
  only if it makes them **all** true.
- The solution of a system is the region where the shadings overlap; a point in
  that region works in every inequality.

These are **real, released Regents questions**.
