Most of algebra comes back to one move: **keep an equation balanced** while you
peel away everything around the variable. Whatever you do to one side, you do to
the other, until the variable stands alone.

- **Solve** linear equations by undoing operations in reverse — clear parentheses
  and fractions, gather the variable on one side, then isolate it.
- **Create** an equation from a word problem by naming the unknown and translating
  the relationship into symbols before you solve.
- Watch for the two special endings: an equation can have **no solution** (a false
  statement like $3 = 5$) or **infinitely many** (a true statement like $0 = 0$).

These are **real, released Regents questions**.
