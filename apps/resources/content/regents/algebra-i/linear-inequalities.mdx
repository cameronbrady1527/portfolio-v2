Solving a linear inequality works just like an equation — **except** that
multiplying or dividing by a negative flips the inequality symbol, and the
solution is a *range* you graph on a number line. A strict symbol (`<` or `>`)
gives an **open** endpoint; `≤` or `≥` gives a **closed** one. These are **real,
released Regents questions** — solve, then pick the graph that matches.
