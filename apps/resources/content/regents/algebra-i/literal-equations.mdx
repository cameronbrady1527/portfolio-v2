A **literal equation** is an equation made mostly of letters — a formula like
$A = \ell w$ or $P_1 V_1 = P_2 V_2$. **Solving** one means rearranging it to get a
chosen variable by itself, with everything else on the other side.

The moves are exactly the moves you already use on number equations — you just
carry the other letters along:

- Undo operations in reverse order, doing the same thing to both sides.
- To free a variable that is **multiplied** by something, divide by it; to free
  one that is **added**, subtract.
- The answer is an expression, not a number — the variable in terms of the rest.

These are **real, released Regents questions**.
