---

A **rational** number can be written as a ratio of two integers — every fraction,
every terminating or repeating decimal. An **irrational** number cannot — its
decimal runs forever with no pattern, like $\sqrt{2}$ or $\pi$.

The Regents questions here are mostly about **closure** — what kind of number you
get back from an operation:

- The sum or product of **two rationals** is always rational.
- The sum of a rational and an **irrational** is irrational; so is the product of
  a **nonzero** rational and an irrational.
- Operations on **two irrationals** are not guaranteed either way — $\sqrt{2}\cdot
  \sqrt{2} = 2$ is rational, but $\sqrt{2}\cdot\sqrt{3} = \sqrt{6}$ is irrational.

These are **real, released Regents questions**.
