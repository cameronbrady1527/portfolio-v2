These are **real, released Algebra I Regents questions** on **data and
statistics**. For a **single data set**, you'll compare **center and spread** —
mean, median, **IQR**, and **standard deviation** — and judge the effect of an
**outlier**. For **two related variables**, you'll find the **line of best fit**
(the linear regression equation) and the **correlation coefficient** $r$, judge a
fit's **strength and direction**, separate **correlation from causation**, and
read a **two-way frequency table**.

For the show-your-work questions, do the calculation (or run your calculator's
regression), then reveal the official NYSED rubric, a worked model solution, and
any figure to score yourself. The multiple-choice items check the *reasoning* —
what the spread tells you, what $r$ means, and whether a link is truly
cause-and-effect.
