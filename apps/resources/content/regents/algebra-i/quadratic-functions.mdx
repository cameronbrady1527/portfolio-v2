Once you can *solve* a quadratic, the next step is reading its **graph — a
parabola**. Three features carry most of the questions:

- **Zeros** (the $x$-intercepts) — where the parabola crosses the $x$-axis. Set
  each factor equal to $0$; a product is zero only when a factor is.
- **The vertex** — the turning point, and the **minimum or maximum** value.
  Rewrite in **vertex form** $f(x) = a(x - h)^2 + k$ by completing the square, and
  the vertex is $(h, k)$.
- **Meaning in context** — for a height-vs-time model, the constant term is the
  **initial height** and a positive zero is **when it lands**.

These are **real, released Regents questions**; read each carefully.
