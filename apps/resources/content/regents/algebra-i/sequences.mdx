A **sequence** is a list of numbers following a rule — a function whose inputs are
the term numbers $1, 2, 3, \dots$. Two kinds dominate Algebra I:

- **Arithmetic** — a constant **common difference** $d$: you *add* $d$ each step.
  The $n$th term is $a_n = a_1 + (n - 1)d$.
- **Geometric** — a constant **common ratio** $r$: you *multiply* by $r$ each step.
  The $n$th term is $a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{\,n-1}$.

You'll also switch between a **recursive** rule (how to get the next term from the
last one) and an **explicit** rule (the $n$th term directly). These are **real,
released Regents questions**.
