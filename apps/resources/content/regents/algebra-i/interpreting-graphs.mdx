A graph tells a story. Before you compute anything, you can *read* it — where the
curve climbs, levels off, or falls, and where it peaks or bottoms out.

- **Relating a graph to an event:** a rising segment means the quantity is
  growing, a falling segment means it's shrinking, and a flat segment means it's
  **constant** (unchanging) — not zero.
- **Key features:** the intervals where a function is **increasing**,
  **decreasing**, or **constant**; where it is **positive** or **negative**; its
  **maximum** or **minimum**; and its **intercepts**.

The graph is given — your job is to interpret it. These are **real, released
Regents questions**.
