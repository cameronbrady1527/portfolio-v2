A **dilation** resizes a figure from a fixed point, called the
<Term id="center-of-dilation">center of dilation</Term>, by a **scale factor**
$k$. Every point moves along the ray from the center, so its distance from the
center is multiplied by $k$. A dilation must always be described with *both*
pieces: the center and the scale factor.

## Dilating about the origin

<Refresher id="ratios" />

A scale factor behaves like a ratio: it **multiplies**, it never adds. When the
center is the origin, the rule is simply

$$
(x, y) \mapsto (kx,\, ky)
$$

With $k = 2$, the point $(2, 3)$ lands on $(4, 6)$ — twice as far from the
origin, in the same direction. A factor $k > 1$ **enlarges** the figure; a
factor between $0$ and $1$ **reduces** it.

Watch the side lengths in the figure above as you drag the scale factor: every
length is multiplied by $k$, so with $k = 2$ each side of the image is exactly
twice its preimage side.

## What a dilation preserves

<PredictThenCheck
  prompt="You double a triangle with a dilation (k = 2) about the origin. What happens to its perimeter?"
  choices={["It doubles", "It stays the same", "It quadruples", "It depends on the triangle"]}
>
  <p>
    The perimeter <strong>doubles</strong>. Every side length is multiplied by
    the scale factor, and the perimeter is just the sum of the sides — so it
    scales by the same factor, for any triangle. (Area is the surprise: it
    scales by k² = 4.) The one thing that doesn't budge is the angles.
  </p>
</PredictThenCheck>

A dilation is **not** a <Term id="rigid-motion">rigid motion</Term>: unless
$k = 1$, distances change, so the image is not <Term id="congruent">congruent</Term>
to the preimage. But the image is always the **same shape** — every angle keeps
its measure — which makes the image <Term id="similar">similar</Term> to the
original.

Two more facts you can check experimentally on the figure:

- A line that passes **through the center** of dilation maps onto itself.
- A line that does **not** pass through the center maps to a **parallel**
  line.
