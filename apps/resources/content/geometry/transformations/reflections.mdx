A **reflection** is a transformation that flips a figure across a line, called
the _line of reflection_. The original figure and its image are mirror images:
every point moves to the opposite side of the line, the same distance away.

## Reflecting over the axes

Reflecting a point over the **x-axis** keeps its $x$-coordinate and negates its
$y$-coordinate:

$$
(x, y) \mapsto (x, -y)
$$

Reflecting over the **y-axis** does the opposite — it negates the $x$-coordinate
and keeps $y$:

$$
(x, y) \mapsto (-x, y)
$$

Because a reflection preserves distances and angles, the image is **congruent**
to the original. So a segment $\overline{AB}$ and its image $\overline{A'B'}$
always have the same length.
