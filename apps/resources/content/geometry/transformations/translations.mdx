A **translation** is a transformation that slides every point of a figure the
same distance in the same direction. Nothing rotates and nothing flips — the
figure simply glides to a new position, so the image stays **congruent** to the
original.

## Translating by a vector

The slide is described by a _translation vector_ $\langle a, b \rangle$, where
$a$ is the horizontal shift and $b$ is the vertical shift. Applying it to a
point adds the components coordinate-wise:

$$
(x, y) \mapsto (x + a,\, y + b)
$$

For example, translating the point $(1, 1)$ by $\langle 3, -2 \rangle$ moves it
to $(4, -1)$. Because the **same** vector is applied to every vertex, the shape
keeps its size, its orientation, and the order of its vertices — only its
location changes.
