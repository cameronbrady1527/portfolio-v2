---
title: base angles
subject: geometry
---

The base angles of an isosceles triangle are the two angles that sit on the base
— the angles opposite the two equal sides. They are always equal to each other.

**Formal definition.** In an isosceles triangle, the two angles opposite the
congruent sides. The Base Angles Theorem states they are congruent, and its
converse holds too: equal base angles force the opposite sides to be equal.
