---
title: triangle inequality
subject: geometry
---

The triangle inequality is the rule that decides whether three lengths can form
a triangle at all: any two sides, added together, must be longer than the third.
If they aren't, the short sides can't reach across to meet.

**Formal definition.** For side lengths $a$, $b$, $c$ of a triangle,
$a + b > c$, $a + c > b$, and $b + c > a$. Three lengths form a triangle exactly
when all three hold; equality gives a degenerate (flat) triangle.
