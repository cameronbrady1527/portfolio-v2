---
title: similar
subject: geometry
---

Two shapes are similar when they have the same shape but not necessarily the
same size — one is a scaled copy of the other. All the angles match, and the
side lengths stay in proportion.

**Formal definition.** Two figures are similar if and only if some sequence of
rigid motions and dilations maps one onto the other. Equivalently, their
corresponding angles are equal and their corresponding side lengths share a
common ratio — the scale factor.
