Add the `what-is-a-proof` proof family — the simplest on-ramp to the two-column form: a figureless algebraic proof that, given a linear equation, proves `x = k` justified line-by-line by the Properties of Equality. Pure and seeded like every `ProofFamily`, with structural variation across seeds (one-/two-/three-step forms; Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication, Division, and Distributive properties; varied integer coefficients and solutions) and level-gated distractors. Registered in the `generateProof` family map and exported from the `/logic` and `/logic/geometry` barrels. The accompanying `geometry/proofs/what-is-a-proof.mdx` page mounts the figureless `ProofBuilder` for this family.
